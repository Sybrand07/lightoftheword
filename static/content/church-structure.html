A Bible-Based Model for Church

1. Church Leadership Structure

The Bible provides a clear framework for how churches should function, ensuring Christ remains at the center.

A. Jesus as the Head of the Church

The ultimate leader of the church is not a pastor, elder, or bishop—it is Jesus Christ.

📖 Ephesians 1:22-23 – “And God placed all things under his feet and appointed him to be head over everything for the church, which is his body, the fullness of him who fills everything in every way.”

📖 Colossians 1:18 – “And he is the head of the body, the church; he is the beginning and the firstborn from among the dead, so that in everything he might have the supremacy.”

🔹 Practical Example: A church where the pastor's word is treated as absolute truth, even when it contradicts Scripture, is out of order. If a pastor claims, "God told me, and you must obey," but it goes against the Bible, they are usurping Christ’s authority.

 

B. Elders/Pastors as Spiritual Overseers

Pastors and elders serve as shepherds, guiding the flock with wisdom and humility.

📖 1 Timothy 3:1-7 – Qualifications of elders.

📖 1 Peter 5:2-3 – “Be shepherds of God’s flock that is under your care, watching over them—not because you must, but because you are willing, as God wants you to be; not pursuing dishonest gain, but eager to serve; not lording it over those entrusted to you, but being examples to the flock.”

📖 Titus 1:7-9 – “Since an overseer manages God’s household, he must be blameless—not overbearing, not quick-tempered, not given to drunkenness, not violent, not pursuing dishonest gain. Rather, he must be hospitable, one who loves what is good, who is self-controlled, upright, holy and disciplined.”

🔹 Practical Example: Elders are not to rule with an iron fist but lead by example, with humility and love. A pastor who demands absolute loyalty and punishes those who question him is acting like a dictator, not a shepherd. A biblical pastor, however, would welcome accountability and correction.

 

C. Deacons as Servant-Leaders

Deacons serve under the elders to handle practical matters of the church, so that pastors can focus on teaching the Word and prayer.

📖 Acts 6:2-4 – “So the Twelve gathered all the disciples together and said, ‘It would not be right for us to neglect the ministry of the word of God in order to wait on tables. Brothers and sisters, choose seven men from among you who are known to be full of the Spirit and wisdom. We will turn this responsibility over to them and will give our attention to prayer and the ministry of the word.’”

🔹 Practical Example: In a healthy church, deacons might organize finances, care for the needy, and maintain the church facilities, allowing the pastor to focus on preaching and discipleship.

 

2. How Congregants Should Relate to Leaders

Church members are called to respect and submit to godly leadership while ensuring leaders remain accountable.

📖 Hebrews 13:17 – “Obey your leaders and submit to them, for they are keeping watch over your souls, as those who will have to give an account. Let them do this with joy and not with groaning, for that would be of no advantage to you.”

📖 1 Thessalonians 5:12-13 – “Now we ask you, brothers and sisters, to acknowledge those who work hard among you, who care for you in the Lord and who admonish you. Hold them in the highest regard in love because of their work.”

🔹 Practical Example: If a pastor rebukes someone in love for living in sin, the congregation should not rebel against correction but prayerfully consider it. However, if a pastor is abusive or manipulative, the church should not blindly submit but should address the issue biblically.

 

3. Qualifications of a Biblical Leader

A true church leader must embody godly character and integrity.

  Blameless (Above Reproach) – 1 Timothy 3:2

  Faithful to Their Family – 1 Timothy 3:2, 1 Timothy 3:4-5

  Self-Controlled and Disciplined – Titus 1:8

  Not Greedy or Materialistic – 1 Peter 5:2

  Able to Teach Sound Doctrine – Titus 1:9

  Not a New Believer (Mature in Faith) – 1 Timothy 3:6
  Humble and Not Arrogant – 1 Peter 5:3

 

📖 1 Timothy 3:1-7 – “Here is a trustworthy saying: Whoever aspires to be an overseer desires a noble task. Now the overseer is to be above reproach, faithful to his wife, temperate, self-controlled, respectable, hospitable, able to teach, not given to drunkenness, not violent but gentle, not quarrelsome, not a lover of money. He must manage his own family well and see that his children obey him, and he must do so in a manner worthy of full respect. (If anyone does not know how to manage his own family, how can he take care of God’s church?) He must not be a recent convert, or he may become conceited and fall under the same judgment as the devil. He must also have a good reputation with outsiders, so that he will not fall into disgrace and into the devil’s trap.”

📖 Titus 1:9 – “He must hold firmly to the trustworthy message as it has been taught, so that he can encourage others by sound doctrine and refute those who oppose it.”

📖 1 Peter 5:2-3 – “Be shepherds of God’s flock that is under your care, watching over them—not because you must, but because you are willing, as God wants you to be; not pursuing dishonest gain, but eager to serve; not lording it over those entrusted to you, but being examples to the flock.”

🔹 Practical Example: A leader who is greedy for money or involved in scandals does not meet biblical qualifications. If a pastor is known for mistreating his wife or being dishonest in business, he is unfit for leadership.

 

4. Dangers of Pastors Who Become ‘God’

Some pastors elevate themselves as the ultimate authority, making themselves spiritual dictators (‘gods’) in the eyes of the congregation.

📖 Ezekiel 34:2-4 – “Woe to you shepherds of Israel who only take care of yourselves! Should not shepherds take care of the flock? You eat the curds, clothe yourselves with the wool and slaughter the choice animals, but you do not take care of the flock. You have not strengthened the weak or healed the sick or bound up the injured. You have not brought back the strays or searched for the lost. You have ruled them harshly and brutally.”

📖 Matthew 23:8-10 – “But you are not to be called ‘Rabbi,’ for you have one Teacher, and you are all brothers. And do not call anyone on earth ‘father,’ for you have one Father, and he is in heaven. Nor are you to be called instructors, for you have one Instructor, the Messiah.”

📖 2 Thessalonians 2:3-4 – The Antichrist is described as one who exalts himself as God, a warning against pastors who do the same.

🔹 Practical Example: A true pastor points people to Jesus, not to themselves. When a pastor controls every aspect of people’s lives—dictating who they should marry, what jobs they should take, and making them fear leaving the church—that is spiritual abuse. Some pastors demand absolute obedience and discourage questioning their teachings. Others seek extravagant wealth and live in excess while their congregation struggles. Some make themselves the center of the church, overshadowing Christ.

 

5. The Opposite Extreme: No Leadership Authority

On the opposite extreme, some churches avoid leadership altogether, making pastors more like consultants than spiritual leaders.

📖 Hebrews 13:7 – "Remember those who rule over you, who have spoken the word of God to you, whose faith follow, considering the outcome of their conduct."

📖 1 Timothy 5:17 – "Let the elders who rule well be counted worthy of double honor, especially those who labor in the word and doctrine."

📖 2 Timothy 4:2-3 – “Preach the word; be prepared in season and out of season; correct, rebuke and encourage—with great patience and careful instruction. For the time will come when people will not put up with sound doctrine.”

📖 1 Corinthians 14:40 – “But everything should be done in a fitting and orderly way.”

🔹 Practical Example: A church where "everyone is equal" in a way that rejects authority can lead to confusion and disunity. If a pastor preaches against sin but the congregation says, "We’ll take it as advice, not a command," then biblical discipline is lost. Churches become disorganized and prone to false teachings. As a result, the church loses its mission and effectiveness.

 

Conclusion: A Balanced Church Structure

The church should avoid both extremes:

  1. Authoritarianism, where the pastor becomes the supreme authority.
  2. Anarchy, where there is no real leadership.

Instead, leadership should be based on servant leadership, where the pastor guides the flock in humility under Christ.

A biblical church structure is one where:

✅ Christ is the head, and all leadershiup is subject to Him.
✅ Pastors/Elders guide with humility and biblical integrity.
✅ Deacons serve to meet practical needs.
✅ Congregants honor leaders but test all teachings by Scripture.
✅ Pastors are not worshiped nor disregarded but respected as shepherds under Christ.

📖 1 Corinthians 11:1 – “Follow my example, as I follow the example of Christ.”

👀 Reflection Questions:

  • Does my church leadership align with biblical principles?
  • Am I submitting to godly authority while testing everything by Scripture?
  • Have I seen either extreme—pastor worship or total rejection of leadership?
