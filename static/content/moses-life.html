Exodus 2: Significant Moments in Moses' Life

Moses' life is generally divided into three significant 40-year periods based on biblical accounts and Jewish tradition. Here's a breakdown of his age at key events:

1. Birth and Early Life in Egypt (0–40 years)

  • Birth and Infancy (Exodus 2:1-10)
    • Moses was born to Amram and Jochebed (Numbers 26:59), a Hebrew couple from the tribe of Levi, during a time when Pharaoh had ordered the killing of all Hebrew baby boys (Exodus 2:1-2).
    • His mother hid him for three months, then placed him in a basket in the Nile River. Pharaoh’s daughter found him and adopted him, naming him "Moses" (which means "drawn out" of the water).
    • His sister, Miriam, arranged for their mother to nurse him until he was weaned, after which he was raised in Pharaoh’s palace as an Egyptian prince (Exodus 2:10).
  • Grows Up as an Egyptian Prince (Acts 7:21-22)
    • Moses was educated in all the wisdom of the Egyptians and was powerful in speech and action.
  • Kills an Egyptian and Flees to Midian (Exodus 2:11-15)
    • Around the age of 40 (Acts 7:23), Moses saw an Egyptian beating a Hebrew slave and killed the Egyptian.
    • When Pharaoh found out, Moses fled to the land of Midian.

2. Life in Midian (40–80 years)

  • Settles in Midian and Marries Zipporah (Exodus 2:16-22)
    • Moses arrived in Midian and defended the daughters of Jethro (Reuel), a Midianite priest, from shepherds at a well.
    • Jethro gave him his daughter, Zipporah, in marriage, and they had a son named Gershom.
    • Moses spent 40 years in Midian as a shepherd (Acts 7:30).
  • God Calls Moses at the Burning Bush (Exodus 3:1-22)
    • At 80 years old (Exodus 7:7), God appeared to Moses in the burning bush on Mount Horeb and called him to lead the Israelites out of Egypt.

3. Leading Israel from Egypt to Canaan (80–120 years)

  • The Ten Plagues and Exodus (Exodus 7–12)
    • Moses and his brother Aaron (who was 83 years old, Exodus 7:7) confronted Pharaoh.
    • After the ten plagues, Pharaoh finally let the Israelites go.
  • Crossing the Red Sea and Receiving the Law (Exodus 14–20)
    • Moses led the Israelites through the Red Sea and into the wilderness.
    • He received the Ten Commandments and the Law at Mount Sinai.
  • Wandering in the Wilderness (Numbers & Deuteronomy)
    • Due to the Israelites' disobedience, they wandered in the desert for 40 years.
    • Moses led them, interceded for them, and received additional laws from God.
  • Death at 120 Years Old (Deuteronomy 34:1-7)
    • At 120 years old, Moses climbed Mount Nebo, where God showed him the Promised Land.
    • He died there, and God Himself buried him in an unknown location.

Summary of Moses’ Age at Key Events

  1. Born – Hidden as an infant (Exodus 2:1-3).
  2. 40 years old – Killed an Egyptian and fled to Midian (Exodus 2:11-15, Acts 7:23).
  3. 80 years old – God called him at the burning bush (Exodus 3:1-12, Acts 7:30).
  4. 80–120 years old – Led Israel out of Egypt and through the wilderness.
  5. 120 years old – Died on Mount Nebo before entering the Promised Land (Deuteronomy 34:7).
