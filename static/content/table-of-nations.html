Descendants of Japheth (Genesis 10:2-5) – Settled in Europe & Asia

Japheth’s descendants spread into Europe, Central Asia, and parts of the Middle East.

Biblical Name Modern Nations (Most Probable) Notes
Gomer Germany, Ukraine, Poland, Russia, Turkey (Anatolia), Armenia Associated with the Cimmerians, who lived in the Black Sea region. May also be linked to Celts.
Magog Russia, Ukraine, Kazakhstan, Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan Linked to the Scythians of Central Asia and Southern Russia.
Madai Iran, Afghanistan, Pakistan The Medes, who later joined the Persians to form the Persian Empire.
Javan Greece, Cyprus, Italy (Magna Graecia), Turkey (Western coast) The Ionians, early Greek settlers.
Tubal Turkey (Anatolia), Georgia, Spain, Russia (Siberia) Associated with the Iberians (Spain/Portugal) or Tobolsk region of Russia.
Meshech Turkey (Anatolia), Russia (Moscow), Georgia Linked to the Mushki tribe in Anatolia; some connect it to Moscow (Russia).
Tiras Bulgaria, Romania, Turkey (Thrace region), Serbia The Thracians, an Indo-European people from the Balkans.

Descendants of Ham (Genesis 10:6-20) – Settled in Africa & the Middle East

Ham’s descendants spread through North Africa, the Levant (Middle East), and parts of Arabia.

Biblical Name Modern Nations Notes
Cush Sudan, Ethiopia, Eritrea, Somalia, Yemen The Kushite kingdom (Nubia) flourished in Sudan & Ethiopia. Some link Cush to southern Arabia (Yemen).
Mizraim Egypt, Sudan The Hebrew name for Egypt.
Put (Phut) Libya, Algeria, Tunisia, Morocco, Mauritania Generally associated with North Africa (Berbers, Numidians, Carthaginians).
Canaan Israel, Palestine, Lebanon, Jordan, Syria The ancestor of the Canaanite tribes, including the Phoenicians (Lebanon & Syria).

Descendants of Shem (Genesis 10:21-31) – Settled in the Middle East & South Asia

Shem’s descendants lived in the Middle East, Arabia, and parts of Asia.

Biblical Name Modern Nations Notes
Elam Iran The Elamites, an early civilization in southwest Iran.
Asshur Iraq, Syria, Turkey The Assyrians, who built an empire in northern Mesopotamia.
Arpachshad Israel, Palestine, Iraq, Iran, Saudi Arabia Ancestor of Eber (Hebrews).
Lud Turkey (Lydia), Italy (Etruscans) Associated with the Lydians (Western Turkey), some link them to Etruscans (Italy).
Aram Syria, Lebanon, Iraq, Jordan The Arameans, ancestors of the Syriac people.
