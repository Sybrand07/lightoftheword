How Godly Men Deal with a Contentious Wife

Godly men throughout history have approached the challenge of dealing with contentious wives in various ways, often with wisdom, patience, and a desire to preserve peace in the marriage while maintaining godly principles. Here are some ways in which they have dealt with contentious spouses:

1. Patience and Endurance

Many men, recognizing the importance of peace in marriage, have shown patience and endurance in the face of a contentious wife. In Proverbs 19:11, it says, —A man's wisdom gives him patience; it is to his glory to overlook an offense.— Godly men often bear with their wives, hoping to guide them with understanding rather than reacting impulsively.

  • Example: The biblical patriarchs, like Abraham, showed patience with their wives' challenges. Abraham endured his wife Sarah's moments of doubt and impatience, even in situations where his faith was tested (such as the issue of Hagar and the promise of a son). He navigated these situations with patience and trust in God's timing.

2. Kind and Gentle Leadership

Ephesians 5:25 calls husbands to "love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave Himself up for her." This kind of sacrificial love requires humility, gentleness, and a servant-hearted approach. Even when faced with a contentious spouse, Godly men strive to lead in a way that is loving, gentle, and kind, not harsh or domineering.

  • Example: King Solomon himself, despite his own failures, was known for his wisdom. He sought peace and reconciliation in his relationships, as evidenced in the story of the two women who claimed the same baby (1 Kings 3:16-28). Solomon's wisdom didn't just deal with conflict but worked to bring clarity, peace, and understanding.

3. Prayer and Seeking God's Help

Prayer has always been a powerful tool for Godly men. When faced with challenging relationships, prayer is seen as the avenue to seek God's wisdom and guidance. Godly men pray for their wives' hearts to be softened, for peace to be restored, and for their own hearts to be changed as well.

  • Example: Hannah in the Old Testament prayed fervently for a child and endured criticism and provocation from her rival, Peninnah. Her response was not to argue but to bring her pain and frustration to God in prayer. Similarly, many Godly men have followed this example, turning to God in times of marital conflict.

4. Leading by Example

Godly men aim to model Christlike behavior, hoping that their actions will encourage their wives to respond in a similar way. When a husband remains calm, kind, and steady in the face of conflict, it has the potential to break the cycle of contention.

  • Example: Job, despite the immense suffering and turmoil in his life, did not lash out at his wife when she advised him to curse God (Job 2:9-10). He modeled humility, restraint, and trust in God, which demonstrated a response grounded in faith rather than in his circumstances.

5. Seeking Wise Counsel

Godly men often turn to trusted advisors, pastors, or mentors when facing difficult marital issues. They understand that seeking wisdom is important and that sometimes external help is needed to provide perspective and guidance.

  • Example: In the Old Testament, King David had the prophet Nathan to offer counsel, especially when he was in sin. For a man in a contentious relationship, seeking godly counsel is often an important way to gain insight into the best way forward.

6. Addressing the Root of the Issue

Sometimes contentiousness stems from deeper issues, like unresolved personal struggles, unmet needs, or past wounds. Godly men will look for ways to address these deeper issues in a loving and supportive manner. They may need to engage in open communication, therapy, or work on healing past hurts.

  • Example: Joseph and Mary, though not a contentious marriage, show the strength of God's intervention in difficult circumstances. Joseph was initially going to divorce Mary quietly (Matthew 1:19), but when he received guidance from an angel, he chose to support her. This illustrates how sometimes external intervention (whether divine or wise counsel) is necessary to help resolve conflicts.

7. Understanding and Empathy

Godly men often strive to understand the heart of their wives and the reasons behind their contentious behavior. Instead of reacting to the surface-level issue, they seek empathy and understanding, knowing that often conflict comes from deeper emotional or spiritual struggles.

  • Example: Peter in 1 Peter 3:7 encourages husbands to live with their wives in an understanding way, recognizing their equal dignity and worth. He advises men to honor their wives as co-heirs of the grace of life, underscoring the importance of respect and empathy in marriage.

Conclusion:

In history, Godly men have recognized that dealing with a contentious wife isn't simply about "fixing" the issue, but about applying the principles of love, patience, wisdom, prayer, and empathy. They know that marriage is a partnership that requires commitment to understanding, leading by example, and seeking God's guidance. By living out these principles, they hope to not only change the dynamics of the relationship but also foster growth and healing for both spouses.
