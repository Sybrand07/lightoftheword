Exodus 4: Why Did God Want to Kill Moses?

The passage where God seemingly seeks to kill Moses is found in Exodus 4:24-26. This is a puzzling and dramatic event that occurs as Moses is on his way back to Egypt after being called by God to deliver the Israelites.

The Passage (Exodus 4:24-26)

"At a lodging place on the way, the LORD met Moses and was about to kill him. But Zipporah took a flint knife, cut off her son's foreskin and touched Moses' feet with it. ‘Surely you are a bridegroom of blood to me,’ she said. So the LORD let him alone. (At that time she said ‘bridegroom of blood,’ referring to circumcision.)" (NIV)

Context of the Story

Moses had just been commissioned by God through the burning bush (Exodus 3-4) to return to Egypt and demand Pharaoh to let the Israelites go. God had assured Moses of divine power and guidance, yet this incident happens immediately after his calling.

Why Did God Want to Kill Moses?

  1. Moses Had Neglected the Covenant of Circumcision
    • Circumcision was a fundamental sign of the Abrahamic Covenant (Genesis 17:9-14). Every male descendant of Abraham was required to be circumcised on the eighth day after birth.
    • Moses, as a Hebrew, should have ensured his own son was circumcised, but he had neglected this duty. This was a direct violation of God's command.
  2. A Leader Must Be Obedient to God's Covenant
    • Moses was about to become the leader of Israel, the spokesman of God, and the mediator of the covenant between God and His people.
    • If Moses was not faithful in obeying the covenant himself, how could he represent God and lead a covenant people?
  3. God’s Holiness and Justice Required It
    • God had chosen Moses, but even chosen people are not exempt from God's commands.
    • God's holiness demanded obedience, and by neglecting circumcision, Moses was in direct disobedience.
  4. Zipporah’s Role in Saving Moses
    • Zipporah, Moses' Midianite wife, quickly intervened by circumcising their son herself, thereby fulfilling the requirement of the covenant.
    • Her reaction—calling Moses a "bridegroom of blood"—suggests that she may have initially resisted the practice of circumcision but realized its necessity.

Theological Implications

  • God’s Calling Requires Full Obedience: Before Moses could deliver Israel, he had to first align himself fully with God’s covenant.
  • God’s Mercy Prevails: Though God sought to kill Moses, He relented once the act of obedience was performed.
  • Leadership Comes with Responsibility: A leader cannot expect followers to obey God if they themselves are neglecting His commands.

Conclusion

God wanted to kill Moses because he had neglected to circumcise his son, a serious violation of the Abrahamic Covenant. As a leader chosen to represent God, Moses had to be in full obedience before carrying out God's mission. This passage serves as a powerful reminder of the seriousness of God's commands and the necessity of obedience, especially for those in positions of spiritual leadership.
