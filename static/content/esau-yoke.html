When Was Esau's Yoke Broken?

Genesis 27:40 (KJV) states:

"And by thy sword shalt thou live, and shalt serve thy brother; and it shall come to pass when thou shalt have the dominion, that thou shalt break his yoke from off thy neck."

This prophecy speaks of Esau’s descendants (Edom) being under Israel's rule and later breaking free.

Historical Fulfillments

Under Israelite Rule – King David (c. 1000 BC)

2 Samuel 8:14 records that David conquered Edom and placed garrisons in their land, fulfilling the prophecy that Esau would serve Jacob.

Breaking the Yoke – Edom’s Rebellion (c. 850 BC)

During King Jehoram’s reign (2 Kings 8:20-22), Edom revolted against Judah and established its own king, marking the moment Esau's descendants broke free.

The Hasmonean Conquest (c. 130 BC)

John Hyrcanus I later conquered Edom (Idumea), integrating them into Jewish society. By the Roman era, Edom had fully merged with Israel, and its distinct identity disappeared.

Summary

The yoke was broken around 850 BC when Edom gained independence, but over time, Esau’s lineage was absorbed into Israel, ceasing to exist as a separate nation.
