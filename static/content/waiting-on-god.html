Is God Waiting on Us, or Are We Waiting on Him?

This is a deep and important question, touching on faith, obedience, patience, and the sovereignty of God. Let's explore it theologically with Scripture.

1. Is God Waiting on Us, or Are We Waiting on Him?

Many believers struggle with whether they should be taking action or waiting on God for breakthrough. The answer is often both—faith involves active obedience while trusting in God's timing.

Surrender and Obedience (God Waiting on Us)

There are times when breakthrough is delayed because God is waiting for us to align our hearts, surrender our will, or take a step of faith. Consider these Scriptures:

  • James 4:7-8 – "Submit yourselves, then, to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you. Come near to God and he will come near to you."
    → Submission precedes movement. Sometimes, God waits for us to surrender before He moves.
  • Exodus 14:15 – "Then the Lord said to Moses, 'Why are you crying out to me? Tell the Israelites to move on.'"
    → Sometimes, we pray for breakthrough, but God is waiting for us to step forward in faith.
  • Luke 5:4-6 – Jesus said to Simon, “Put out into deep water, and let down the nets for a catch.” Simon answered, “Master, we’ve worked hard all night and haven’t caught anything. But because You say so, I will let down the nets.” When they had done so, they caught such a large number of fish that their nets began to break.
    →     The lesson here is not about mere action but obedience to Jesus' word. Peter had already worked hard all night, yet nothing changed until he acted on Jesus' specific instruction. Breakthrough does not come by human effort alone—God gives the increase.

God’s Timing and Sovereignty (Waiting on Him)

On the other hand, sometimes God calls us to wait, trust, and endure. Consider:

  • Isaiah 40:31 – "But those who wait on the Lord shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles."
    → Waiting is not passive; it strengthens us.
  • Habakkuk 2:3 – "For the vision is yet for an appointed time; but at the end it shall speak, and not lie: though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come, it will not tarry."
    → Some breakthroughs require God's perfect timing.
  • Psalm 37:7 – "Be still before the Lord and wait patiently for him; do not fret when people succeed in their ways."
    → God's plans often unfold slower than we expect, but we must trust Him.

2. What Does It Mean to “Stay Busy” While Waiting?

Waiting in the Bible is not about inactivity; it is about active trust. Consider these ways to remain faithful while waiting:

Faithfulness in Small Things

  • Luke 16:10 – "Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much."
    → Before God gives greater breakthrough, He often tests faithfulness in smaller assignments.

Serving in the Present Moment

  • Colossians 3:23 – "Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord, not for human masters."
    → Whatever work is before you now, do it for the Lord, even while waiting for your next season.
  • Matthew 25:21 – "Well done, good and faithful servant! You have been faithful with a few things; I will put you in charge of many things."
    → Faithfulness in the present leads to increase and promotion in God’s timing.

Seeking God and His Kingdom

  • Matthew 6:33 – "But seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things will be added to you."
    → While waiting for breakthrough, prioritize your spiritual growth, obedience, and serving others.
  • Psalm 127:1 – “Unless the Lord builds the house, the builders labor in vain. Unless the Lord watches over the city, the guards stand watch in vain."
    →     This reminds us that even in our work, God must be the foundation. Staying busy does not mean striving in human effort alone. If we are building our plans instead of aligning with God’s, our labor is in vain.

Prayer and Fasting

  • Philippians 4:6 – "Do not be anxious about anything, but in every situation, by prayer and petition, with thanksgiving, present your requests to God."
    → Use waiting seasons to deepen your relationship with God through prayer.

Encouraging Others

  • Galatians 6:9 – "Let us not become weary in doing good, for at the proper time we will reap a harvest if we do not give up."
    →     Waiting seasons are opportunities to bless others, knowing that God will bring harvest in due time.

3. How Do You Ensure You Are Building the Lord’s House and Not Your Own?

A key question in seasons of action is whether we are pursuing God's will or simply our own ambitions.

Self-Examination and Motives

  • Psalm 139:23-24 – "Search me, O God, and know my heart; test me and know my anxious thoughts. See if there is any offensive way in me, and lead me in the way everlasting."
    → Regularly ask God to check your heart's motives.
  • Proverbs 16:3 – "Commit to the Lord whatever you do, and he will establish your plans."
    → Ensure your work is surrendered to God, not driven by pride.

Are You Advancing the Kingdom or Personal Gain?

  • Haggai 1:4-6 – "Is it a time for you yourselves to be living in your paneled houses, while this house remains a ruin? Now this is what the Lord Almighty says: ‘Give careful thought to your ways.'"
    → The Israelites prioritized their own houses over God's temple. We must evaluate whether our work is truly for His glory.
  • 1 Corinthians 3:11-13 – "For no one can lay any foundation other than the one already laid, which is Jesus Christ. If anyone builds on this foundation using gold, silver, costly stones, wood, hay or straw, their work will be shown for what it is, because the Day will bring it to light."
    → Our work will be tested—make sure it aligns with God's eternal purpose.

Measuring by the Fruit of the Spirit

  • Galatians 5:22-23 – "But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, forbearance, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness and self-control."
    → If your work is led by God, it will produce spiritual fruit.

4. Conclusion: Balancing Waiting and Action

  • If God has given you a clear instruction, obey in faith (Luke 5:5-6).
  • If you are uncertain, focus on faithfulness in the present, while seeking God’s direction (Psalm 127:1).
  • If it is a season of waiting, trust God’s perfect timing, knowing that He is always working behind the scenes (Habakkuk 2:3).
  • Above all, seek first His Kingdom, ensuring that whatever you do is for His glory, not self-promotion (Matthew 6:33).
