Exodus 20: What is meant by exposing your nakedness?

Exodus 20:26 (ESV) says:
"And you shall not go up by steps to my altar, that your nakedness be not exposed on it."

Context of Exodus 20:26

This verse appears right after the giving of the Ten Commandments on Mount Sinai. In this section, God is providing additional instructions on how to build and approach His altar. The immediate context (Exodus 20:24-25) includes instructions on making altars out of earth or uncut stones, emphasizing simplicity and humility in worship.

Cultural and Historical Context

In ancient Israel, priests often wore simple, long garments that could expose parts of the body when climbing stairs. God instructed that altars should not have steps so that no one’s nakedness would be exposed while ascending to make sacrifices.

In contrast to the practices of surrounding pagan cultures—where priests would often wear scanty or elaborate garments and use elevated altars—God set a clear standard of modesty and reverence.

Spiritual Significance

  1. Holiness and Modesty:
    God’s instructions emphasized the holiness required when approaching Him. Unlike the pagan practices that often included immodesty and sensuality, God’s worship was to be pure and reverent.
  2. Respect for Human Dignity:
    God’s concern for modesty reveals His desire to protect human dignity, even in sacred spaces. This reflects the consistent biblical theme that the human body, created by God, is to be honored.
  3. Simplicity in Worship:
    Unlike the grand, elaborate altars of other nations, Israel’s altars were to be made of earth or uncut stones. This showed that God did not require grandiosity but genuine worship and obedience.
  4. Symbol of Humility:
    The avoidance of steps also symbolizes humility before God. The altar should not be a place of human exaltation but one of sacrifice and submission.

Broader Biblical Themes

  • Modesty in Worship:
    The principle of modesty in worship continues throughout Scripture. In the New Testament, Paul instructs believers to dress modestly and respectably (1 Timothy 2:9).
  • Holiness and Approach to God:
    The idea that worshippers must carefully prepare to approach God appears throughout the Bible. The high priest, for example, had to perform extensive purification rituals before entering the Holy of Holies (Leviticus 16).
  • Contrast with Pagan Worship:
    Pagan worship often involved sensuality and exposure (as seen in the worship of Baal and Asherah). God set Israel apart by emphasizing holiness, modesty, and humility.

Application for Today

  1. Worship with Reverence:
    When approaching God in worship, we should do so with a sense of reverence and respect, being mindful of how we present ourselves both outwardly and inwardly.
  2. Humility Over Pride:
    The absence of steps to the altar is a reminder that worship is not about exalting oneself but humbly acknowledging God’s greatness.
  3. Modesty Reflects Inner Attitude:
    Modesty is not just about clothing but about the attitude of the heart—a humble posture before God, acknowledging that He is holy.

Final Thoughts

Exodus 20:26 highlights God’s concern for purity and humility in worship. While the practical instruction was specific to ancient Israel, the principle of reverence when approaching God is timeless. It reminds us that worship is not a spectacle but a humble and sincere offering to a holy God.
