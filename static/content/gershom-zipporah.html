Exodus 2: What happened to Gershom and Zipporah?

Gershom and Zipporah, the son and wife of Moses, appear in different parts of the Bible, but their stories after Moses’ departure to Egypt are somewhat obscure. Here’s a detailed look at what happened to them based on biblical texts and historical traditions.

 

Zipporah (Moses' Wife)

Zipporah was the daughter of Jethro (also called Reuel), the priest of Midian. She married Moses when he fled from Egypt and lived in Midian (Exodus 2:15-22). She bore him at least two sons, Gershom and Eliezer (Exodus 18:3-4).

The Mysterious Incident in Exodus 4

One of the most cryptic events concerning Zipporah occurs in Exodus 4:24-26, when Moses was on his way back to Egypt. The passage states that the Lord sought to kill Moses, but Zipporah quickly circumcised their son and touched Moses with the foreskin, calling him a "bridegroom of blood." This act seemed to appease God's wrath, suggesting that Moses had neglected the covenant of circumcision, and Zipporah took immediate action.

Because of this event, it is believed that Moses sent Zipporah and their sons back to her father, Jethro, in Midian before proceeding to Egypt to confront Pharaoh.

Reunion with Moses

Later, in Exodus 18, after the Israelites had left Egypt and were in the wilderness, Jethro brought Zipporah and her two sons to meet Moses. This implies that she had been living with her father during the Exodus. Jethro then advised Moses on how to delegate leadership among the Israelites.

Her Fate After Exodus 18

The Bible does not explicitly mention Zipporah after this encounter. Some theories suggest:

  1. She stayed with Moses for the rest of his life, though no further details are provided.
  2. She may have returned to Midian with Jethro, considering that Moses later married a "Cushite woman" (Numbers 12:1), which some interpret as Zipporah, while others suggest it was a different woman.
  3. Rabbinic and extra-biblical traditions suggest that she may have lived among the Israelites but was not mentioned again due to the primary focus on Moses' leadership.

 

Gershom (Moses' Firstborn Son)

Gershom was the firstborn son of Moses and Zipporah. His name means "I have been a foreigner in a strange land" (Exodus 2:22), reflecting Moses’ experience in Midian.

Role in Israel’s History

  • Unlike his father, Gershom did not take on a prominent leadership role in Israel.
  • He is mentioned in 1 Chronicles 23:15-17, where his descendants are noted.
  • His son Shebuel (or Shubael) is later mentioned as a ruler in King David’s administration over temple treasuries (1 Chronicles 26:24), meaning Gershom's lineage was involved in priestly and Levitical service.

Controversy – Was Gershom’s Line Corrupted?

  • Judges 18:30 mentions a priest named Jonathan, son of Gershom, son of Moses (some manuscripts say "son of Manasseh").
  • This Jonathan became a priest for idolatrous worship in the tribe of Dan, which had turned away from true worship of Yahweh.
  • Some scholars argue that Gershom’s lineage may have strayed into idolatry, though others suggest that the text may have been edited to preserve Moses' reputation.

 

What Happened to Them in the End?

  1. Zipporah – Likely remained with Moses or returned to Midian; her fate is uncertain.
  2. Gershom – His descendants had a role in temple service, but some may have been associated with idolatry.

While their direct fates are not extensively detailed, their legacy continued through their descendants, shaping later biblical history.
