Luke 24: How Jesus Expounded the Scriptures to Reveal Himself

In Luke 24:27, Jesus began at Moses and all the Prophets, showing how the entire Hebrew Scriptures pointed to Him. To help a Jewish person understand how the Hebrew Scriptures (Tanakh) reveal Jesus as the Messiah, let's explore some of the key prophecies and their fulfillment in Jesus:

 

1. The Seed of the Woman – Genesis 3:15

Scripture:
"And I will put enmity between you and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel." (Genesis 3:15)

Explanation:
This is the first prophecy of the Messiah. God promised that a descendant of the woman (not the man) would crush the head of the serpent (Satan), though He Himself would be wounded in the process.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus was born of a virgin (Isaiah 7:14, Matthew 1:23), making Him uniquely the "seed of the woman."
  • On the cross, Satan bruised His heel (causing temporary suffering), but through His resurrection, Jesus crushed the serpent’s head (defeating sin and death).

 

2. The Sacrificial Lamb – Genesis 22:8

Scripture:
"And Abraham said, ‘My son, God will provide Himself a lamb for a burnt offering.’" (Genesis 22:8)

Explanation:
When Abraham was commanded to offer Isaac on Mount Moriah, God intervened and provided a substitute—a ram caught in a thicket. This foreshadowed the ultimate sacrifice of God’s Son, Jesus.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus is called the Lamb of God (John 1:29).
  • He was sacrificed on a mountain near Moriah—the same region where Abraham offered Isaac.

 

3. The Passover Lamb – Exodus 12:5-13

Scripture:
"They shall take a lamb without blemish… and they shall take some of the blood and put it on the two doorposts… when I see the blood, I will pass over you." (Exodus 12:5-13)

Explanation:
During the first Passover, Israel was saved from death by the blood of a lamb on their doorposts. This was a foreshadowing of Jesus, who would shed His blood for the salvation of mankind.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus died on Passover (Matthew 26:2).
  • He was without blemish—sinless (1 Peter 1:19).
  • His blood saves us from eternal death (1 Corinthians 5:7).

 

4. The Rock That Gave Living Water – Exodus 17:6

Scripture:
"Behold, I will stand before you there on the rock in Horeb; and you shall strike the rock, and water will come out of it, that the people may drink." (Exodus 17:6)

Explanation:
Moses struck a rock, and water flowed out, saving Israel in the wilderness. This symbolized Jesus, the true Rock, who would be struck for our salvation and give us living water.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Paul writes, "That Rock was Christ." (1 Corinthians 10:4)
  • Jesus said, "If anyone thirsts, let him come to Me and drink." (John 7:37)

 

5. The Bronze Serpent – Numbers 21:9

Scripture:
"So Moses made a bronze serpent and set it on a pole; and if a serpent had bitten anyone, when he looked at the bronze serpent, he lived." (Numbers 21:9)

Explanation:
When Israel sinned, God sent venomous snakes as judgment. Yet, He also provided a way of salvation: a bronze serpent lifted on a pole. Anyone who looked at it in faith was healed.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus said, "As Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up, that whoever believes in Him may have eternal life." (John 3:14-15)
  • Just as the bronze serpent took the curse of sin, Jesus took our sins on the cross.

 

 

6. The Tabernacle – God's Dwelling Among His People (Exodus 25-40)

Scripture:
"And let them make Me a sanctuary, that I may dwell among them." (Exodus 25:8)

Explanation:
The Tabernacle was a temporary dwelling where God's presence met with Israel. Every element pointed to Jesus, who would dwell among us permanently as Emmanuel, "God with us" (Isaiah 7:14).

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • "The Word became flesh and tabernacled among us." (John 1:14)
  • The Holy of Holies, where God's presence rested, was separated by a veil—but when Jesus died, the veil was torn (Matthew 27:51), showing that access to God was now open through Christ.

 

7. The High Priest – Leviticus 16 (Day of Atonement/Yom Kippur)

Scripture:
"The high priest shall make atonement for the Most Holy Place because of the uncleanness and rebellion of the Israelites, whatever their sins have been." (Leviticus 16:16)

Explanation:
On Yom Kippur, the High Priest entered the Holy of Holies once a year to atone for Israel's sins with the blood of a spotless sacrifice. This foreshadowed Jesus, our Great High Priest, who offered His own blood once for all.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • "But Christ came as High Priest… not with the blood of goats and calves, but with His own blood, He entered the Most Holy Place once for all, having obtained eternal redemption." (Hebrews 9:11-12)
  • Jesus is now our eternal High Priest (Hebrews 4:14-16).

 

8. The Scapegoat – Leviticus 16:10

Scripture:
"But the goat on which the lot fell to be the scapegoat shall be presented alive before the Lord, to make atonement upon it, and to let it go as the scapegoat into the wilderness." (Leviticus 16:10)

Explanation:
On Yom Kippur, two goats were chosen:

  • One was sacrificed for sin.
  • The other, the scapegoat, was sent into the wilderness, symbolizing the removal of sin.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus took our sins upon Himself (Isaiah 53:6).
  • Like the scapegoat, He was led outside the city (Hebrews 13:12) and took away our sins.

 

9. The Prophet Like Moses – Deuteronomy 18:15 (Continued)

Scripture:
"The Lord your God will raise up for you a Prophet like me from among your brethren. Him you shall hear." (Deuteronomy 18:15)

Explanation:
Moses was Israel’s greatest leader, delivering them from slavery and giving them the Law. But he prophesied that a greater Prophet would come, one who would fully reveal God’s will.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Like Moses, Jesus spoke with God face to face (John 1:18).
  • Like Moses, Jesus performed miracles (Matthew 11:4-5).
  • Like Moses, Jesus delivered His people from slavery—but not from Egypt, from sin (John 8:34-36).
  • When God spoke from heaven at the Transfiguration, He said: “This is My beloved Son… Hear Him!” (Matthew 17:5), directly referencing Deuteronomy 18:15.

 

10. The Curse for Hanging on a Tree – Deuteronomy 21:23

Scripture:
"His body shall not remain overnight on the tree, but you shall surely bury him that day, so that you do not defile the land… for he who is hanged is accursed of God." (Deuteronomy 21:23)

Explanation:
The Law stated that anyone hung on a tree was under God’s curse. This foreshadowed Jesus, who bore the curse of sin for us when He was crucified.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • “Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law, having become a curse for us (for it is written, ‘Cursed is everyone who hangs on a tree’).” (Galatians 3:13)
  • Jesus was crucified, taking upon Himself our punishment, so that we might receive God’s blessing instead of judgment.

 

11. The Conquering Lion from Judah – Genesis 49:10

Scripture:
"The scepter shall not depart from Judah, nor the ruler’s staff from between his feet, until Shiloh comes; and to Him shall be the obedience of the peoples." (Genesis 49:10)

Explanation:
Jacob prophesied that the Messiah would come from the tribe of Judah and would rule the nations. This pointed to Jesus, the King of Kings.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus is called the Lion of Judah (Revelation 5:5).
  • He is the eternal King (Luke 1:32-33).
  • Through Him, all nations will be blessed and obey Him (Matthew 28:18-20).

 

12. The Son of David – 2 Samuel 7:12-13

Scripture:
"I will establish the throne of His kingdom forever." (2 Samuel 7:12-13)

Explanation:
God promised David that one of his descendants would rule forever. The Jews expected a conquering king, but first, the Messiah had to come as a suffering servant.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus is the Son of David (Matthew 1:1).
  • He will return as King to establish His eternal kingdom (Revelation 11:15).

 

13. The Shadow of Jonah – A Sign of Resurrection

Scripture:
"For just as Jonah was three days and three nights in the belly of the great fish, so will the Son of Man be three days and three nights in the heart of the earth." (Matthew 12:40, referencing Jonah 1:17)

Explanation:
Jonah was swallowed by a great fish for three days and then came out alive. This was a prophetic foreshadowing of Jesus' death and resurrection.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus was in the tomb for three days.
  • He rose from the dead, just as Jonah came out of the fish.
  • This was the sign given to the Jewish people proving Jesus was the Messiah (Matthew 12:39-40).

 

14. The New Covenant – Jeremiah 31:31-34

Scripture:
"Behold, the days are coming, says the Lord, when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and the house of Judah… I will put My law in their minds and write it on their hearts." (Jeremiah 31:31-33)

Explanation:
The Mosaic Covenant was based on the Law written on stone. But God promised a New Covenant, where His law would be written on hearts.

Fulfillment in Jesus:

  • Jesus established this New Covenant (Luke 22:20).
  • Instead of external laws, we are transformed inwardly through the Holy Spirit (2 Corinthians 3:6).

 

16. The Messiah Must Come Before the Temple is Destroyed (Daniel 9:24-26)

Scripture: “After the sixty-two ‘sevens,’ the Anointed One will be put to death and will have nothing. The people of the ruler who will come will destroy the city and the sanctuary.” (Daniel 9:26)

Explanation: Daniel prophesied that the Messiah would come and be “cut off” (killed) before the destruction of the Second Temple (which happened in 70 AD). Jesus was crucified around 30 AD—exactly in line with Daniel’s prophecy.

Fulfillment: Jesus was rejected, crucified, and the Temple was destroyed soon after, showing that the time for the Messiah had already come.

 

17. The Messiah Will Be Born of a Virgin (Isaiah 7:14)

Scripture: “The virgin will conceive and give birth to a son, and will call Him Immanuel.” (Isaiah 7:14)

Explanation: While some Jewish interpretations suggest the word “alma” means “young woman,” it is significant that the Greek Septuagint translates it as “parthenos” (virgin), meaning the Jewish scholars before Jesus understood it as a miraculous birth.

Fulfillment: “The virgin shall conceive and bear a son, and they shall call His name Immanuel (which means, God with us).” (Matthew 1:23)

 

18. The Messiah Will Be a Light to the Gentiles (Isaiah 49:6)

Scripture: “I will also make You a light for the nations, that My salvation may reach to the ends of the earth.” (Isaiah 49:6)

Explanation: God always planned for the Messiah to bring salvation beyond Israel to all nations. Jesus fulfilled this by commissioning His disciples to spread the Gospel to the whole world.

Fulfillment: “Go therefore and make disciples of all nations.” (Matthew 28:19)

 

19. The Messiah Must Suffer and Die (Isaiah 53)

Scripture: “He was pierced for our transgressions, He was crushed for our iniquities; the punishment that brought us peace was on Him, and by His wounds we are healed.” (Isaiah 53:5)

Explanation: Many Jewish interpretations see this passage as referring to Israel, but Jesus would have pointed out that Isaiah 53 speaks of a single righteous servant who suffers for the sins of others. Jesus fulfilled this when He died on the cross as an atoning sacrifice.

Fulfillment: “This is My blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for the forgiveness of sins.” (Matthew 26:28)

 

20. The Messiah Must Rise from the Dead (Psalm 16:10)

Scripture: “For You will not abandon my soul to Sheol, nor will You allow Your Holy One to see corruption.” (Psalm 16:10)

Explanation: This passage speaks of someone who will not remain in the grave. Jesus would have shown them that King David, who wrote this psalm, died and was buried, but the true “Holy One” would be raised from the dead.

Fulfillment: “God raised Him up, loosing the pangs of death, because it was not possible for Him to be held by it.” (Acts 2:24)

 

Conclusion: The Road to Faith

When Jesus walked with the disciples on the road to Emmaus, He expounded from Moses all the ways the Scriptures pointed to Himself. He showed them that:

  • He is the Seed of the Woman who crushes Satan.
  • He is the Sacrificial Lamb who takes away sin.
  • He is the Living Water and the Rock of Salvation.
  • He is the High Priest, the Scapegoat, and the Final Prophet.
  • He is the Son of David, who will return to reign forever.

Just as those disciples’ hearts burned when Jesus opened the Scriptures to them (Luke 24:32), may the truth of Messiah Jesus ignite faith in those who seek Him today.

"These are the Scriptures that testify about Me." (John 5:39)

 

A Call to Jewish Salvation – What Must We Do?

Now that we see how Jesus fulfills the Hebrew Scriptures, how should we respond?

  1. Repent and Believe – Recognize that Jesus is the promised Messiah and trust in Him for salvation (Mark 1:15).
  2. Be Washed by His Blood – Just as the sacrifices in the Torah atoned for sin, Jesus is the final sacrifice (Hebrews 9:22, John 1:29).
  3. Receive the New Covenant – Allow God to write His law on your heart and give you a new spirit (Ezekiel 36:26-27).
  4. Confess Him as Lord – If we acknowledge Jesus as Lord and believe in His resurrection, we will be saved (Romans 10:9).

This is the message Jesus expounded, and it remains the path to eternal life.
