How to Complain in a Godly Manner

Complaining is a complex issue from a spiritual perspective because the Bible presents both warnings against grumbling and examples of faithful lament. Let’s explore the how, when, and whether it’s appropriate to complain to people, using Scripture as our guide.

 

1. How to Complain: The Biblical Way

The Bible differentiates between faith-filled lament and faithless grumbling. Here’s how to approach complaining in a godly manner:

a) Be Honest but Reverent

  • Psalm 142:1-2 (NIV) - "I cry aloud to the Lord; I lift up my voice to the Lord for mercy. I pour out before him my complaint; before him, I tell my trouble."
    David was honest about his distress, but he directed it toward God, showing reverence even in his pain.

b) Anchor Your Complaints in Faith

  • Habakkuk 1:2 (NIV) - "How long, Lord, must I call for help, but you do not listen? Or cry out to you, ‘Violence!’ but you do not save?"
    Habakkuk complained about the state of his nation, but his complaint was not rooted in rebellion or disbelief. Instead, it came from a place of faith, seeking understanding from God.

c) End with Hope or Trust

  • Psalm 13:5-6 (NIV) - "But I trust in your unfailing love; my heart rejoices in your salvation."
    David often ended his complaints with a declaration of faith, acknowledging that despite his feelings, God remains faithful.

 

2. When to Complain: Discerning the Right Time

Complaining becomes sinful when it is:

  • Persistent and Ungrateful: (Numbers 11:1) The Israelites’ constant complaints were met with God’s anger because they disregarded His past faithfulness.
  • Born from Bitterness: (Hebrews 12:15) Bitterness defiles and leads to grumbling that harms the spirit.

When Is It Appropriate?

  • In Genuine Suffering: It’s appropriate to lament when you are genuinely hurting (Job 3:1).
  • Seeking Guidance: When you need clarity or intervention (Psalm 22:1-2).
  • In Righteous Anger: When injustice grieves your spirit (Psalm 94:3-4).

When Is It Inappropriate?

  • Out of Ingratitude: Forgetting God’s past works (Exodus 16:2-3).
  • To Stir Up Division: Complaining to sow discord among others (Proverbs 6:16-19).
  • Without Purpose: Complaining just to vent anger without seeking a solution (Philippians 2:14).

 

3. Should One Ever Complain to People?

Complaining to people can be appropriate when it is done:

  • With Humility: Sharing a burden without blaming others (Galatians 6:2 - "Bear one another’s burdens").
  • With the Right Intent: Seeking counsel or comfort rather than spreading negativity (Proverbs 15:22).
  • To Those Who Can Help: Like sharing issues with mentors, pastors, or supportive friends who will pray or offer godly wisdom (James 5:16).

When Not to Complain to People:

  • Gossip or Slander: Complaining about someone behind their back (Ephesians 4:29).
  • Seeking Pity or Validation: When the goal is to gain sympathy rather than solve a problem (Philippians 4:6).
  • To the Wrong Audience: Sharing personal struggles with those who cannot or should not be involved (Matthew 18:15).

 

4. Spiritual Wisdom: What’s the Motive?

Before complaining, ask yourself:

  • Am I seeking help or just venting?
  • Will my words honor God or stir up division?
  • Am I seeking solutions or just spreading negativity?
  • Will this lead me closer to God or further into doubt?

 

Summary: The Heart Matters

Complaining is not inherently sinful, but the motive, attitude, and approach make a difference. Lamenting with honesty, humility, and a desire to grow closer to God is biblical. However, grumbling from a place of bitterness or rebellion displeases God.
When sharing complaints with others, ensure it is done in a way that builds up rather than tears down, and that it ultimately points back to trusting in God.
