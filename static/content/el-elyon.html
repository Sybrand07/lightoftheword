God's Character: El Elyon

In Genesis 14:18, the phrase used for "God Most High" in Hebrew is אֵל עֶלְיוֹן (El Elyon).

  • אֵל (El) — A common Semitic word for "God."
  • עֶלְיוֹן (Elyon) — Meaning "Most High" or "Exalted."

The name El Elyon (אֵל עֶלְיוֹן, "God Most High") appears multiple times in the Old Testament, emphasizing God's supreme authority. Here are some key passages:

Genesis

  1. Genesis 14:18-20 — Melchizedek, king of Salem, blesses Abram in the name of El Elyon, calling Him the "Possessor of heaven and earth."
  2. Genesis 14:22 — Abram responds to the king of Sodom, affirming that he has sworn an oath to El Elyon.

Deuteronomy

  1. Deuteronomy 32:8 — "When the Most High (Elyon) gave the nations their inheritance, when He divided mankind..."
    • This verse highlights God's sovereignty in distributing the nations.

Psalms

  1. Psalm 7:17 — "I will praise the Lord according to His righteousness: and will sing praise to the name of the Lord Most High (YHWH Elyon)."
  2. Psalm 9:2 — "I will be glad and exult in You; I will sing praise to Your name, O Most High (Elyon)."
  3. Psalm 18:13 — "The Lord thundered from heaven, and the Most High (Elyon) uttered His voice."
  4. Psalm 21:7 — "For the king trusts in the Lord, and through the steadfast love of the Most High (Elyon) he shall not be moved."
  5. Psalm 46:4 — "There is a river whose streams make glad the city of God, the holy habitation of the Most High (Elyon)."
  6. Psalm 50:14 — "Offer to God a sacrifice of thanksgiving, and perform your vows to the Most High (Elyon)."
  7. Psalm 57:2 — "I cry out to God Most High (El Elyon), to God who fulfills His purpose for me."
  8. Psalm 78:35 — "They remembered that God was their rock, the Most High God (El Elyon) their redeemer."
  9. Psalm 83:18 — "That they may know that You alone, whose name is the Lord, are the Most High (Elyon) over all the earth."
  10. Psalm 91:1 — "He who dwells in the secret place of the Most High (Elyon) shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty."
  11. Psalm 92:1 — "It is good to give thanks to the Lord, to sing praises to Your name, O Most High (Elyon)."

Prophets

  1. Isaiah 14:14 — The fall of Lucifer: "I will ascend above the heights of the clouds; I will make myself like the Most High (Elyon)."
  2. Lamentations 3:35 — *"To turn aside the right of a man before the face of the Most High (Elyon)."

Daniel

  1. Daniel 3:26 — Nebuchadnezzar calls Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego "servants of the Most High God (Elah Illaya in Aramaic, equivalent to El Elyon)."
  2. Daniel 4:17, 24-25, 32, 34 — Nebuchadnezzar acknowledges that the Most High (Elyon) rules over the kingdoms of men.
  3. Daniel 5:18, 21 — Belshazzar is reminded that his father, Nebuchadnezzar, was humbled by the Most High (Elyon).
  4. Daniel 7:18, 22, 25, 27 — The saints of the Most High (Elyon) will receive the kingdom.

Summary of Themes

  • God's Supreme Authority — He rules over all creation (Gen. 14:19, Deut. 32:8, Dan. 4:17).
  • God as Protector and Refuge — He is a shelter for His people (Ps. 91:1).
  • God's Sovereignty in Judgment and Nations — He controls history and kingdoms (Dan. 4:34, Ps. 46:4).
  • God as the Object of Worship — He alone is worthy of praise (Ps. 92:1, 83:18).
